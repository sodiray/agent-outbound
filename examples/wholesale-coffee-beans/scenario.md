# Scenario: Wholesale Coffee Beans to Independent Coffee Shops

## The Business

We're a small specialty coffee roaster based in Boise, Idaho called Sawtooth Roasting Co. We roast single-origin and custom blend beans and sell wholesale to independent coffee shops, cafes, and restaurants. We do our own local delivery within the Treasure Valley — the Boise metro area — and our roastery is off Chinden Blvd in Garden City.

We've been in business for two years. We have a handful of existing wholesale accounts but we're ready to aggressively expand our local reach. We know the product is good — every shop that's tasted our roasts has switched or added us. The bottleneck is getting in front of decision-makers.

## The Offer

A **free, no-commitment tasting session**. We bring sample roasts to the shop and let the owner or manager taste them side by side with whatever they're currently using. No pitch deck, no minimum order commitment. Just coffee.

If they like what they taste:
- No minimum order. Weekly delivery. They can start with one bag and scale up.
- We handle the switch logistics — they tell us what flavor profile they want, we dial in the blend, we show up every Tuesday with fresh bags.
- Competitive wholesale pricing ($9–14/lb depending on origin and volume).

The tasting session is the conversion event. Everything in the pipeline is designed to get us into the shop with sample bags in hand.

## ICP — Who We're Targeting

**Geography:** Boise metro — Boise, Eagle, Meridian, Nampa, Garden City, Star, Kuna. Caldwell is a stretch but worth including. Everything within about 25 miles of our roastery.

**Vertical:** Independent coffee shops and cafes. Not chains (Starbucks, Dutch Bros, Black Rock, Human Bean). Not bakeries that happen to have a drip pot. Not restaurants where coffee is an afterthought. The coffee has to be core to what they do.

**Ideal fit:**
- Owner-operated or has an identifiable manager who makes purchasing decisions
- Espresso and/or specialty drip coffee is central to their offering
- Has a real web presence — a website, active social media, shows up in local search. This signals that the owner is engaged and accessible.
- Has been open at least a year (startups churn; established shops are worth the time)
- Not visibly locked into a roaster partnership already (e.g., their site doesn't say "Proudly serving Doma Coffee" or "Roasted by Thomas Hammer")

**Disqualifiers:**
- National or regional chain/franchise
- No physical storefront (online-only, farmers market only)
- Primarily a restaurant, bar, or bakery where coffee is a side item
- Already a Sawtooth account

## Trigger Signals — What Makes Outreach Urgent

- **Actively hiring baristas** → they're growing, possibly opening a new location, good time to get in

- **Recently opened or under new ownership** → decisions are being made right now
- **Active social media posting** → engaged owner who's accessible and cares about the brand
- **No visible roaster partnership on their website** → they're either unbranded or quiet about it, which means they might be open to switching

## The Outreach Sequence

Three touches over seven days. Email-first, with an in-person visit as the closer.

**Day 0 — Intro email.** Personalized to the owner by name. Reference something specific we found about their shop — a menu item, their Instagram, a review, their vibe. Introduce ourselves as a local roaster, pitch the free tasting, keep it casual. We're neighbors, not a sales team.

**Day 3 — Follow-up email.** Same thread. Add a new angle — maybe a seasonal roast we're excited about, or a mention of a nearby shop in their neighborhood we already work with (social proof), or a riff on something they posted recently. Short. The goal is to feel like a real person following up, not an automation.

**Day 7 — In-person visit.** Walk into the shop. Ask for the owner or manager. If they're there, introduce ourselves and offer to leave sample bags for them to try on their own time. If they're not, leave a sample bag and a one-page flyer with our info and a QR code to book the tasting. Either way, log the disposition.

The visit is timed for day 7 so there's been two email touchpoints first. The owner may not have replied but they've seen our name twice. When we walk in and say "Hey, I'm Ray from Sawtooth Roasting — I sent you an email last week about doing a tasting," there's a chance of recognition.

## Why This Scenario Tests the Full Pipeline

Every phase of agent-outbound matters for this campaign:

- **Sourcing** needs to find real coffee shops across multiple cities, exclude chains, handle dedup across Google Maps and web results
- **Enrichment** needs to find the owner, their email, what's on their website, whether they're hiring, social media activity — all of which feed scoring and personalized outreach
- **Scoring** fit and trigger descriptions directly map to the ICP and timing signals above — the scores should reflect real judgment about whether a shop is worth pursuing and when
- **Sequencing** is a real multi-channel local sales motion with a concrete offer — email personalization requires enrichment data, visit scheduling requires territory config
- **Visits** are the conversion event — the tasting is where the deal happens
- **Compliance** matters — we're cold-emailing local businesses and showing up in person; suppression, CAN-SPAM, and do-not-knock are real concerns
